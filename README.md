# Eko-Sustainable-Clothing-Brand-Strategy-Analysis

Project Overview

This project focuses on building a sustainable clothing brand from scratch and conducting a comprehensive marketing and strategic analysis. The brand created, Ekoé, is positioned as a premium yet accessible sustainable fashion label for eco-conscious consumers.

The study integrates market research, strategic frameworks, consumer insights, branding, and digital marketing execution to evaluate the feasibility and positioning of Ekoé in the competitive Indian fashion market.

Brand Concept: Ekoé

Brand Type: Sustainable fashion and lifestyle brand

Tagline: Wear the Change

Core Idea: Blending timeless design with ethical sourcing and sustainability

Target Market: Urban, eco-aware consumers aged 22–45 with mid to high disposable income

Key Objectives

Create a brand identity from scratch

Analyze the external environment and industry structure

Understand consumer behavior and willingness to pay for sustainable fashion

Define clear segmentation, targeting, and positioning (STP)

Develop product, pricing, channel, and communication strategies

Design dashboards and visual insights using Excel

Positioning Statement:
For conscious professionals and style-forward changemakers, Ekoé offers ethically crafted, premium clothing that blends timeless design with environmental integrity.

Strategy Highlights
Product Strategy

Capsule collections to reduce waste

Use of sustainable fabrics: organic cotton, linen, bamboo silk

Pricing Strategy

Tiered premium pricing

Products priced approximately 10–20% above conventional fashion

Channel Strategy

Digital-first approach

Focus on online platforms and Instagram

Future expansion via pop-ups and retail collaborations

Communication Strategy

Storytelling around sustainability and craftsmanship

Transparency, certifications, and behind-the-scenes content

Dashboard & Visual Analysis

Consumer channel preference analysis

Willingness-to-pay insights

Purchase frequency trends

Competitive benchmarking

Visual storytelling dashboards created in Excel

Key Insights

A significant portion of consumers are willing to pay a 10–15% premium for sustainable clothing

Online platforms dominate purchase decisions for conscious buyers

Brand storytelling and transparency are critical differentiators

Sustainability works best when positioned as a lifestyle, not just a product feature
